About Me

Hello! I'm Maharab Hossen, a developer and researcher passionate about building intelligent, scalable, and impactful technologies. Currently, I am pursuing my undergraduate degree in the Department of Artificial Intelligence, majoring in Computer Science and Technology, and I am expected to graduate in 2027.

My primary research interests are System Architecture and Intelligent Computing, where I explore how modern computing systems can be designed to be scalable, efficient, and intelligent. I am particularly interested in developing architectures that bridge advanced AI with real-world applications, enabling reliable, high-performance systems capable of solving complex challenges.

My work spans artificial intelligence, Large Language Models (LLMs), distributed AI systems, and intelligent software engineering. I enjoy building AI-driven applications while exploring LLM fine-tuning, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), AI agents, model optimization, and the deployment of open-source foundation models. I am especially interested in how these technologies can be integrated into scalable system architectures to create robust, adaptive, and production-ready intelligent systems.

I believe that the future of AI lies not only in developing more capable models but also in designing the architectures that enable them to operate efficiently, reliably, and at scale. My goal is to contribute to research that advances intelligent computing infrastructures while translating cutting-edge AI innovations into practical solutions with real-world impact.

Why Me

Research Focus: My research bridges AI algorithms and system-level optimization. I have published work on deep reinforcement learning for intelligent traffic control (under review with Oxford University Press), where I designed a multi-agent RL framework that dynamically optimizes signal phases across urban intersections. I am also building a quantized LLM inference engine capable of running 70B-parameter models on consumer GPUs (RTX 5070Ti) through extreme model compression, custom CUDA kernels, and memory-aware offloading strategies. This work sits at the intersection of AI and systems — exactly where next-generation intelligent computing is heading.

Technical Depth: I specialize in the full stack of intelligent system development. On the algorithm side, I work with PyTorch, TensorRT, and custom training pipelines for computer vision and LLM tasks. On the systems side, I optimize CUDA kernels, design compiler passes for quantized inference, and build ROS2-based autonomous perception stacks. My projects demonstrate deployment on severely resource-constrained hardware — from Jetson Nano edge devices to consumer GPUs running trillion-parameter workloads. I have hands-on experience with distributed training, containerized deployment (Docker), and real-time embedded systems, giving me the end-to-end perspective needed for production-grade AI infrastructure.

Why China: Having studied at Nanjing Tech University and won national robotics competitions in China (27th China Robot and AI Competition — National Third Prize, Provincial Second Prize), I am deeply familiar with Chinese academic culture, research expectations, and the innovation ecosystem. I am drawn to top-tier Chinese institutions like SJTU and USTC for their world-leading research in AI systems, high-performance computing, and intelligent computing infrastructure. My multilingual background (Bengali native, English professional, Chinese conversational) and cross-cultural adaptability position me to thrive in collaborative, international research environments. I am committed to contributing to China's advancement in AI systems research while building bridges between global academic communities.
